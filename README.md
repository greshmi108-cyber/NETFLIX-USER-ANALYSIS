# 🎬 Netflix Growth & User Engagement Analytics Platform

**An end-to-end growth analytics project** — synthetic-data simulation of a
Netflix-style subscription streaming business, covering acquisition, engagement,
retention, churn, and revenue analysis using **SQL, Python, and Power BI**.

> Built to reflect how a Growth/Product Analyst at a subscription streaming
> company would actually approach this problem — not a classroom exercise.
> All data is synthetically generated (not real Netflix data) but modeled on
> realistic SVOD industry patterns and benchmarks.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Netflix-style subscription businesses live or die by four numbers: how many
users they acquire, how deeply those users engage, how many they retain, and
how much revenue each one generates. This project builds the full analytics
stack to answer those questions — from raw relational data, through SQL and
Python analysis, to an executive-ready Power BI dashboard — and closes with 25
concrete recommendations a Growth leadership team could act on.

**Scale:** 11 relational tables, **1,077,000+ records**, 4.5 years of simulated history (2022–2026).

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Netflix's Growth and Product teams need a unified view connecting acquisition →
engagement → retention → revenue to:
- Reduce blended CAC and improve LTV:CAC ratio
- Catch at-risk subscribers before they churn, not after
- Tie content investment to measurable engagement outcomes
- Give Growth and Product a shared, governed KPI language

Full write-up: [`Documentation/01_Business_Problem.md`](Documentation/01_Business_Problem.md)

## 🔍 Key Business Insights

1. Cohort retention stabilizes after month 3 — the first 90 days are the critical
   intervention window for onboarding and retention programs.
2. "Price too high" and "Not enough content" together account for ~48% of stated
   cancellation reasons — signaling a pricing/value-perception problem, not just a churn problem.
3. Engagement depth (watch time, title breadth) correlates with revenue more than
   raw tenure does — watch-time nudges protect LTV more than time-based loyalty alone.
4. Genre popularity is highly region-dependent — a single global content strategy
   leaves engagement on the table.
5. A small "Power User" segment drives disproportionate watch time and is the
   highest-value target for referral and upsell campaigns.
